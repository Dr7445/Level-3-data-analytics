Description of the open datasets that can be used for analysis:


 

Open datasets are collections of data that are freely accessible in the public domain for use ,reuse and redistribution and are typically stored in digital format and are usually stored in a digital format.

These datasets are traditionally released by governments, institutions such as healthcare and education establishments, organisations and researchers.

Some examples of open datasets that are in the public domain include.

 

  • The world bank - Financial institution that provides loans and grants to governments.

 

  • DEFRA- Department for environment, food and rural affairs, DEFRA makes freely available data relating to food production, the environment and agricultural affairs.  

 

  • ONS (office of national statistics)- The UK's office of statistics for the economy, society and general population

 

  • Data.gov.uk-  Uk government project to make freely available data for various public sectors including healthcare and education

 

  • NASA-  National Aeronautics and Space Administration has an open data portal for public use.



These are all entities that release datasets in to the public domain.

Open datasets foster an environment of transparency and public engagement that can be beneficial to society as a whole.
